What you’re probably thinking

The honest answers.

Why not just watch YouTube?

YouTube teaches developers. The best AI tutorials online are written by people who already know how to code — their version of easy is your version of what does that even mean? I learned this from your side of the screen, not theirs. The shortcuts I take are the ones that work for someone with a P&L to run, not a computer science degree.

What if I’m completely non-technical?

So am I. I can’t write a for loop. I can ship products. The gap between can’t code and can build software used to be three years and a CS degree. Now it’s a Thursday evening and a willingness to try.

Will this really replace a hire?

A senior marketer is £80k. A junior developer is £60k. Most of what they do in their first six months is now a Claude Code prompt and an afternoon. Not all of it — you’ll still hire one day. But probably later, for higher-leverage work, and with a clearer brief.

Won’t AI ruin everything?

Probably. In the meantime, founders who learn to wield it will eat the ones who don’t. That’s the only honest thing anyone can tell you about this right now.

Why a cohort, not a course?

Courses go stale in three months. Claude Code ships new things every Tuesday. Live sessions stay current. So do the prompts you take home. And the room beats the recording — watching another founder ship the thing you were stuck on is worth more than any module.

Will this conflict with your day job at Regeno?

One Thursday a week, evenings. A Slack channel. A monthly call. I built half the things on this page around Regeno. The whole point is that it doesn’t need to be a full-time job — yours or mine.

Can I cancel?

Any time. No notice, no clawback. Everything you’ve built and every recording you’ve seen stays yours. The only thing you lose is access to next Thursday.
