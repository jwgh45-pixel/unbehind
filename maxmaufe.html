Pre-call brief

Hi Max. Read this before our call.

A short page to keep us aligned before the brief call. Should take you about 10 minutes. Jot anything down as you go — bring your reactions on Zoom.

The call has two jobs: (1) understand your business well enough that I can spot the AI flows that’d actually move the needle, and (2) agree on the first thing we’d build together.

30 minutes · Free · Zoom link to follow

What I already know

The starting point.

This is what I’m bringing into the call — correct me where I’m wrong, fill the gaps where I’m thin.

  • You’re a headhunter
  • You want more leads
  • That’s it — the rest is what we’ll unpack
Questions I’ll ask

The seven things I need to understand.

Don’t pre-write answers — just have them in your head. We’ll move quickly.

A The business

  • What sectors and seniority do you recruit in?
  • Retained, contingent, or both?
  • Solo, or with a team / assistants / researchers?
  • Rough numbers: average placement fee, placements per month, run-rate.

B ‘More leads’ — which kind?

  • Candidates (people to place), clients (companies who’ll hire), or both?
  • If you had to pick one as the bigger bottleneck right now — which?
  • What does ‘a lead’ mean for you? A name? A meeting? A signed JD?

C How leads come in today

  • Candidates: LinkedIn Recruiter, referrals, inbound, your own database?
  • Clients: cold outreach, referrals, content, network, BD partner?
  • What’s actually working? What’s broken or feels embarrassing?
  • If a lead came in tomorrow, walk me through what happens in the first hour.

D Your week

  • Where does the time actually go? Hours on sourcing, outreach, calls, admin.
  • Which task do you most wish you didn’t have to do?
  • Which task do you wish you had more time for — and what stops you?

E Tools and data

  • ATS / CRM stack? (Bullhorn, Loxo, Vincere, Recruit CRM, Notion, spreadsheet?)
  • LinkedIn Recruiter or Sales Navigator? Other paid sourcing tools?
  • What data is already sitting around — old CV piles, past candidate lists, BD notes?
  • Anything compliance-wise to know upfront (GDPR on candidate data, exclusivity clauses)?

F What good looks like

  • If we double your lead flow next quarter — what does that mean? Volume? Quality? Fees?
  • One number that, if it moved, would feel like a real win.
  • What would ‘great’ look like by the end of the year if this works?

G Constraints

  • Budget — Setup at £500 to start, or thinking about retainer from day one?
  • Timeline — when do you want the first thing live?
  • Anything off-limits? (Data you can’t share, tools you can’t touch, ways of working you won’t accept.)
What I’m already thinking about

Six flows that might fit a headhunter.

These are just starting points. You don’t have to react to all of them — pick the ones that sound right, kill the ones that don’t. The shortlist comes out of the call, not out of this page.

01

Candidate sourcing scraper

You define the niche; the system pulls 30-50 new candidates each morning from LinkedIn with role, location, current company, and a signal that they might move. Lands in a spreadsheet or your inbox.

02

Outreach personaliser

Paste a candidate profile, get a first-message draft in your voice in under 30 seconds. Trained on your best historical outreach. Works for ten at a time.

03

Client target list (overnight)

Companies hiring in your niche, scraped from job boards every night and ranked by likelihood-to-engage-a-headhunter. With the hiring manager’s contact where we can find it.

04

JD-to-shortlist matcher

Paste a job description, get a ranked shortlist from your existing database (or LinkedIn) with a one-paragraph reason for each match. Cuts the ‘who do I have for this?’ step from 90 minutes to 5.

05

CV parser + searchable database

Drop any CV in, structured data out, deduped against your database. Searchable in plain English — “show me UK-based fintech CTOs who’ve raised Series A.”

06

Daily market signal digest

Overnight scrape of funding rounds, exec moves, layoffs and new senior hires in your niche. Lands in your inbox at 7am with the ‘who to call this morning’ list.

After the brief call

How this goes from here.

  1. We do the 30-min Zoom. I take notes. You tell me where I’m wrong.
  2. If it’s a fit, I write up the build spec the same day and send it over.
  3. You approve the spec. I go away and design + build (5-7 days, offline).
  4. We jump on a 60-90 min integration call. I plumb it into your business, you watch it work, we fix what’s broken.
  5. You leave with a working tool. We either stop there, or move to a monthly retainer.
